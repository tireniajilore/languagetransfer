# Same Product, Different Audience: 13% → 59%

## The second test

After the MBA chat results (31 visitors, 13% start rate, 3% completion, 0 demand), I wasn't sure if the product was the problem or the audience.

So I changed the audience. Shared the same app, same single lesson, in language learning Discord servers.

## The numbers

| Metric | MBA chat | Discord |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Visitors | 31 | ~27 |
| Starts | 4 (13%) | 16 (59%) |
| Completions | 1 (3%) | 6 (22%) |
| Demand submissions | 0 (0%) | 2 (7.4%) |

Same product. Everything changed.

## What this means

**Audience-product fit is real.** The product didn't change between tests. The audience did. MBA classmates are generalist curious. They'll click a link, glance at a page, move on. Discord language learners are looking for exactly this. They click play because they came to learn Spanish.

**22% completion is strong.** One in five people who land on the page finish the entire lesson. For a single-lesson MVP with no onboarding, no account, no gamification, just a lesson that plays... that's real.

**2 demand submissions = 2 real people who want more.** Small sample. But they finished the lesson and voluntarily said "build more of this." That's pull, not push.

## The drop-off

The progress milestone data tells an interesting story. People who don't complete don't even reach 25% progress. They bounce in the first few turns.

This isn't a content problem or a lesson length problem. It's a first impression problem. Something in the first 30 seconds is losing 40% of the people who click play.

Could be load time. Could be the opening prompt assuming context from a previous lesson. Could be the mobile experience. Could be the UI not making it clear what to do.

## What's next

Two things:

**Talk to the 2 demand users.** They raised their hand. A 10-minute call with each of them will teach me more than 200 more anonymous funnel visits. What made them start? What almost made them leave? What would they pay for?

**Understand the first-30-seconds problem.** Why do people who click play leave before getting through 3 turns? That's the biggest lever. Fix that and completion could go from 22% to 35%+.

The product question is settling. People who want to learn Spanish and find this app will use it. The open questions now are: can I fix the first impression, can I scale the content quality, and is there a business here?

Ten days. One lesson. Two people who want more.

That's enough to keep building.
